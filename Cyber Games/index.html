Why Cyber Security?

  1. Protection of Personal Information: Safeguards your personal data like bank details, social security numbers, and personal emails from unauthorized access.
  2. Preventing Identity Theft: Protects you from having your identity stolen and misused, which can lead to significant financial and legal troubles.
  3. Business Continuity: Ensures businesses can operate smoothly without interruptions caused by cyber attacks like ransomware.
  4. Safeguarding Sensitive Data: Prevents sensitive information, such as intellectual property or trade secrets, from being accessed or stolen.
  5. Maintaining Trust: Builds trust with customers and partners by demonstrating a commitment to protecting their information.
  6. National Security: Protects critical infrastructure and government information from cyber threats that could impact national security.

Password Security



  1. Length Matters: Aim for passwords that are at least 12 characters long to enhance security.

  2. Mix it Up: Use a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.

  3. Avoid Common Words: Stay away from easily guessable passwords like "password," "123456," or "qwerty."

  4. Unique for Every Account: Never reuse passwords across different sites or services.

  5. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Adds an extra layer of security by requiring a second form of verification.

Unknown Ads



  • Adjust Privacy Settings: Review and update your privacy settings on browsers and social media platforms to limit ad tracking.

  • Use Ad Blockers: Consider using ad blockers to reduce the number of ads you see online.

  • Clear Cookies: Regularly clearing your cookies can help reduce targeted ads, though it might also affect your browsing experience.

  • Be Cautious with Clicks: Avoid clicking on ads from unknown sources to prevent potential scams or malware.

  • Educate Yourself: Stay informed about common online scams and advertising tactics to recognize suspicious ads.

Installations



  • Security Risks: APK files from unverified sources may contain malware, spyware, or other harmful software that can compromise your device and steal sensitive information.
  • Compatibility Issues: Some APK files might not work properly on your device or Android version, leading to potential instability or crashes.
  • No Automatic Updates: Apps installed via APK files don’t update automatically, so you’ll need to manually track and install updates, which can be inconvenient.
  • Legal Concerns: Downloading paid apps for free or modified versions may breach copyright laws and result in legal consequences.
  • Phishing Threats: Some APK files might be designed to deceive you into providing personal or financial information.
